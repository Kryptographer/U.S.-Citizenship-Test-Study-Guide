About the Civics Test

The Test Format

During your naturalization interview, a USCIS officer will ask you up to 10 questions from the list of 100 civics questions. You must answer 6 out of 10 questions correctly to pass.

Speaking Test

The civics test is an oral test - you must speak your answers. Some questions have multiple acceptable answers, and you only need to provide one correct answer.

Special Considerations

Applicants 65 years or older with 20+ years of permanent residence may study only 20 marked questions (indicated with asterisks).

Interview Day Preparation

What to Bring

  • Green Card (Permanent Resident Card)
  • Passport(s) - current and expired
  • State ID or Driver's License
  • Interview Notice (Form I-797C)
  • Reentry Permits (if applicable)
  • Any documents related to your N-400 application

What to Expect

  • Arrive 15-30 minutes early
  • You'll go through security screening
  • Wait in lobby until your name is called
  • Interview lasts approximately 15-20 minutes
  • Officer will review your N-400 application
  • English reading/writing test before civics

During the Civics Test

  • Listen carefully to each question
  • You can ask the officer to repeat a question
  • Give only ONE correct answer per question
  • If unsure, give your best answer - don't stay silent
  • Test stops once you answer 6 correctly
  • Stay calm and confident - you've prepared!

Power is divided among three branches so no single branch becomes too powerful.

Checks and Balances

  • Reading: Read 1 out of 3 sentences correctly
  • Writing: Write 1 out of 3 sentences correctly

  • Double-check all dates and addresses

Interview Day Success Tips

Before the Interview

  • Review your N-400 application thoroughly
  • Practice the 100 civics questions
  • Practice reading and writing in English
  • Prepare all required documents
  • Get a good night's sleep
  • Plan to arrive 15-30 minutes early

What to Wear

  • Dress professionally and conservatively
  • Business casual is appropriate
  • Avoid political or controversial clothing
  • Be neat and well-groomed

During the Interview

  • Be polite and respectful
  • Listen carefully to each question
  • Answer honestly - never lie
  • Ask for clarification if needed
  • Speak clearly and at a normal pace
  • Bring an interpreter if needed (and allowed)

Common Interview Questions

  • What is your current address?
  • How long have you lived there?
  • Where do you work?
  • Have you traveled outside the U.S.?
  • Have you ever been arrested?
  • Are you willing to take the Oath of Allegiance?

The Oath of Allegiance

If your application is approved, you'll take this oath at your ceremony:

"I hereby declare, on oath, that I absolutely and entirely renounce and abjure all allegiance and fidelity to any foreign prince, potentate, state, or sovereignty, of whom or which I have heretofore been a subject or citizen; that I will support and defend the Constitution and laws of the United States of America against all enemies, foreign and domestic; that I will bear true faith and allegiance to the same; that I will bear arms on behalf of the United States when required by the law; that I will perform noncombatant service in the Armed Forces of the United States when required by the law; that I will perform work of national importance under civilian direction when required by the law; and that I take this obligation freely, without any mental reservation or purpose of evasion; so help me God."
